# DragonSword: Awakening - Is It Worth the $30 Price Tag?

DragonSword: Awakening has been making waves in the gaming community, especially with its bold transition from a free-to-play mobile game to a premium buy-to-play title. With a release date set for July 23rd, 2026, many are wondering if the game is worth the $30 price tag. In this article, we'll delve into the game's features, gameplay, and performance to help you decide if it's the right choice for your gaming library.

- **Critical Tip**: Pay attention to the game's progression system to avoid unnecessary grinding
- **Top Choice**: For those who enjoy anime-style ARPGs and want a complete game without gacha elements

## Introduction to DragonSword: Awakening

DragonSword: Awakening started its journey in South Korea as a free-to-play mobile game with "gotcha" mechanics. However, the game's development team, Hound 13, decided to pivot and relaunch it as a premium buy-to-play game, removing all "gotcha" elements and rethinking the entire progression curve. This move has sparked a lot of interest and debate among the gaming community.

## Gameplay and Mechanics

### Combat System

One of the standout features of DragonSword: Awakening is its combat system. The game offers fast-paced ARPG combat with a tag team system, allowing players to switch between characters on the fly. Each character has unique abilities and playstyles, which adds depth to the combat experience. The game also features status effects and chain combo mechanics, making for a dynamic and engaging gameplay experience.

### Progression System

The progression system in DragonSword: Awakening is a topic of debate. While the game offers a complete roster of 19 heroes unlockable through story progression, the system still carries some of the DNA of the original free-to-play game. This includes features like the karma system and resource management, which can lead to grinding and prolonged engagement.

### Open-World Exploration

The game's open-world exploration is competent, with a variety of environments and spaces to explore. However, some segments feel a bit generic, and the real fun seems to lie in the combat and character swapping.

## Visuals and Performance

### Graphics

The graphics in DragonSword: Awakening are impressive, with a beautiful, colorful anime-style aesthetic. The game uses the Unreal Engine 5, which helps in creating a sense of scale and variety in the environments. The art direction is commendable, with warm palettes and highly polished visual styles that make the world of Orbis feel like a living, breathing place.

### Performance

The game's performance on PC, given its origin as a mobile game, leaves room for improvement. Frame instability, texture pop-in, and camera issues are present, which can be frustrating for long play sessions. However, the game does not exhibit massive crashes, and the overall experience is stable.

## Conclusion

DragonSword: Awakening is a game with a lot of potential, especially for those who enjoy anime-style ARPGs and want a complete game without gacha elements. The combat system is engaging, and the visuals are impressive. However, the progression system and performance issues on PC are areas that need improvement. If you're on the fence, consider playing the demo to see if the game's pacing and combat style resonate with you.
